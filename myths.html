Myths

• Systems are extremely rare

Though not exactly common, a much higher portion of the population are systems than you might expect. DID specifically is estimated to affect 1-2% of the world's population. As systems with DID are only a subset of all systems, the portion of the population who are systems is higher. That means in a group of 100 people, you'd expect there to be at least a few systems!

• Systems all have evil alters or are likely to be violent

Systems are much more likely to be the victims of violence than the other way around. The violent alter myth has been perpetuated by many inaccurate media portrayals of systems. Systems are no more dangerous to be around than singlets are.

• Systems with X trait are all faking and we should call them out

This is often targeted towards systems with many introjects, systems with large alter counts, systems who do or don't switch often/openly, systems who consider their alters more or less separate than "typical"

Broadly, people faking dissociative disorders is a much smaller scale issue than real systems getting harassed, forced into the closet, or being pushed into denying their own system due to fakeclaiming. Systems with any of these traits can definitely be real systems, and fakeclaiming systems does much more harm than good.

• Systems can't be happy while plural, and have to seek final fusion to heal from trauma

Many systems do seek final fusion as part of their healing process, but this is not the only way for a system to work through their trauma and be happy. Eliminating dissociative barriers and amnesia is possible while retaining separate identities between alters, and many systems find this (known as healthy multiplicity) to be a more comfortable goal for them. Whether a particular system seeks final fusion or healthy multiplicity is an entirely personal choice.

• Dissociative disorders are like schizophrenia or delusional disorders, and alters are hallucinations or delusions

Though the experience of people who experience hallucinations and delusions are important to respect and consider, dissociative disorders are a very different set of experiences. Alters are real and have their own thoughts and feelings, and are not delusions or hallucinations.

• All systems know they're systems

DID, OSDD, and P-DID are generally covert disorders, meaning they tend to hide themselves from the person having them and others. It can be difficult to tell the differences between mood swings, general identity confusion, etc from switching. Dissociative amnesia can make this even more difficult. Systems often have difficulties with denial even after they consider the possibility of being a system too. There are many systems who are not aware that they're systems. The limited portrayal of these disorders in media and culture also contributes to this.
